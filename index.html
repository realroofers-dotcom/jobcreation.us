The philosophy

No handouts. Just stimulated opportunity.

Training instead of a check. A hike instead of a hotline. A gala that honors the workers instead of just writing a donation. If you want to get, you have to give — this is a capitalist endeavor, not a handout. Twelve years in, that's still the whole model.

The crisis is deepening: seniors are aging out of work and sleeping in cars all over the country. Keeping people working isn't just an economic goal — it's how they live longer.

RooferSchool.com

Roofing is the springboard, not the ceiling. Beyond the trade, students learn business and investing — how funding actually works — so the skill can grow into a company, not just a job. Every roofer who ever moved on took everything we knew with them — no knowledge held back, no hard feelings. Thousands trained and employed over the years — some built massive roofing businesses of their own, others became actors, one became a priest. A traveling program that brings the classroom city to city.

Visit site → Community · On the road

TrailShrinks.com

A weekly hike where everyone speaks freely on any subject — and everyone listens like a shrink, then responds freely too. No appointment needed. 7,500+ signed-up hikers, with turnouts up to 100 strong on a single hike.

Visit site → Charity · Being revived

TheHikersBall.com

A formal gala with a live band, honoring homeless shelter workers. Formal on purpose — a night to feel important, not just fed or thanked in passing. Admission: a new, clean pair of women's underwear, donated directly to local shelters. Held for years, paused for a time — now coming back, and expanding to more cities.

Visit site →
